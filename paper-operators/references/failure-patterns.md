# Failure Patterns

Use this reference during QA and critique. A beautiful image can still fail.

## Common Failures

### Decorative Operator

Symptom: the operator stands, points, waves, or looks cute while the diagram works without them.

Fix: make the operator physically reveal, route, weigh, block, repair, fold, light, or stamp the core relation.

### Paper-Textured PPT

Symptom: the image is just boxes, arrows, and labels with paper grain.

Fix: turn the relation into a physical scene with depth, props, state, and one operator action.

### Atmosphere Instead Of Story

Symptom: story/book cards show mood and setting but no turning point, choice, cost, or transformation.

Fix: use `story-card-grammar.md` and make the central change visible.

### Fake Data Beauty

Symptom: chart looks polished but values, order, units, or labels are wrong.

Fix: reject. Use `data-story-scenes.md` exact data block or reroute to native chart.

### Reference Hallucination

Symptom: historical, scientific, brand, or object details are plausible but wrong.

Fix: gather reference cues and regenerate; note uncertainty if needed.

### Text Overload

Symptom: the image contains title, subtitle, labels, paragraph, legend, and caption all at once.

Fix: use `layout-handoff.md` and `text-strategy.md`; move title/explanation outside the image.

### Template Lock

Symptom: every topic becomes a route table, room, gallery, or little workflow.

Fix: run creative divergence and Swap Test. The metaphor world must come from this source, not the last successful image.

### Wrong Owner

Symptom: Paper Operators tries to solve a strict chart, full slide layout, full social card, logo, or editable diagram.

Fix: hand the job to native chart, PPT skill, social-card skill, vector/code diagram, or image edit. Paper Operators can still make a supporting reasoning image.

## QA Rule

If one of these failures is present, do not describe the image as "mostly fine." Name the failure and either regenerate or reroute.
