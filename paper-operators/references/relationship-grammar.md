# Relationship Grammar / 关系语法

Every Paper Operators image encodes at least one relationship. Choose the relationship before choosing the operator. Precision comes from rendering the exact relation, direction, condition, and state, not from adding generic arrows.

## Relationship Families

### Connection / 连接
Two things must be linked so the reader sees a shared path, reference, or exchange.
Article signals: "connects", "links", "between A and B", "bridges", "relates to", "把 A 和 B 连起来", "中间那条线".
Visual encoding: passage-blue ribbon, thread, bridge strip, or pinned path between two labeled paper objects; the connection has a visible start, end, and contact point.
Operator family: Thread Runner / 牵线员, Bridge Relay / 接力员, Weaver / 编织员.
Precision ladder: line between objects -> labeled ribbon with direction -> anchored path with source, target, medium, and reason tag.
Degrades into: a plain line or arrow with no reason to exist.

### Sequence & Handoff / 顺序·交接
One stage passes material, ownership, or attention to the next stage.
Article signals: "then", "after", "step", "handoff", "from X to Y", "交给", "下一步", "接力".
Visual encoding: cards on a staged route, each with a small state tag; a relay bridge or tray shows what is handed over.
Operator family: Bridge Relay / 接力员, Thread Runner / 牵线员, Archive Tender / 档案员.
Precision ladder: left-to-right steps -> numbered stations -> visible handoff object plus owner labels and state change at each station.
Degrades into: a timeline with arrows and no ownership change.

### Dependency / 依赖
One element cannot stand, move, or make sense without another.
Article signals: "depends on", "requires", "built on", "without X", "前提是", "依赖", "支撑".
Visual encoding: lower support slab, anchor pin, brace, shelf, or load-bearing tab holding the dependent object in position.
Operator family: Stack Mason / 搭层员, Anchor Pin / 定位员, Gate Builder / 闸门员.
Precision ladder: stacked blocks -> support layer with label -> visible load path, missing-foundation failure state, and dependency condition tag.
Degrades into: a vertical stack that only means "higher/lower".

### Causality & Trigger / 因果·触发
One event activates, releases, or produces another.
Article signals: "because", "therefore", "leads to", "when X happens", "causes", "触发", "导致", "一旦".
Visual encoding: latch, falling tab, lit fuse strip, switch, stamp, or gate trigger that visibly changes the next state.
Operator family: Gate Builder / 闸门员, Valve Keeper / 阀门员, Lens Keeper / 检视员.
Precision ladder: arrow from cause to effect -> trigger object -> condition label, activation moment, before/after pair, and resulting state tag.
Degrades into: a causal arrow that could also mean sequence.

### Feedback Loop / 反馈环
An output returns to influence the input; the loop may amplify or stabilize.
Article signals: "feedback", "loop", "reinforces", "self-correcting", "越...越...", "反过来", "闭环".
Visual encoding: circular ribbon with a return mark; reinforcing loops use growing tabs, balancing loops use a scale, damper, or valve.
Operator family: Pulse Reader / 测脉员, Residue Sweeper / 残留清理员, Valve Keeper / 阀门员.
Precision ladder: circle arrow -> return path with reading -> labeled loop type, signal value, adjustment point, and output state.
Degrades into: a decorative circle arrow.

### Contrast & Opposition / 对比·对立
Two positions, states, or readings must be compared in one frame.
Article signals: "but", "whereas", "instead", "A vs B", "on the other hand", "相比", "反而", "不是...而是".
Visual encoding: split frame, mirrored trays, paired plinths, two light cards, or opposing tags sharing the same scale or reference line.
Operator family: Mirror Comparator / 对照员, Lens Keeper / 检视员, Color Tuner / 调色员.
Precision ladder: two boxes -> paired objects -> same measuring frame, matched labels, contrast axis, and highlighted difference.
Degrades into: two unrelated panels.

### Tradeoff & Balance / 权衡·取舍
Choosing more of one value means less, delay, risk, or cost elsewhere.
Article signals: "tradeoff", "cost", "give up", "balance", "priority", "取舍", "代价", "两难".
Visual encoding: paper scale, counterweights, sliders, score cards, or a tilted decision table with removed and kept tags.
Operator family: Tradeoff Weigher / 权衡员, Mediator / 调停员, Valve Keeper / 阀门员.
Precision ladder: scale with two sides -> weighted objects -> named values, cost tag, chosen side, and residual risk card.
Degrades into: a balanced scale that says only "decision".

### Hierarchy & Containment / 层级·包含
Parts live inside a whole, or smaller layers sit within larger structures.
Article signals: "inside", "contains", "part of", "layer", "system", "belongs to", "包含", "层级", "母体".
Visual encoding: nested trays, shelves, transparent layers, cutaway room, labeled boxes within a larger paper object.
Operator family: Stack Mason / 搭层员, Cartographer / 制图员, Archive Tender / 档案员.
Precision ladder: stacked boxes -> nested containers -> part-whole labels, boundary depth, ownership tags, and one exposed cutaway.
Degrades into: an org chart or pile of boxes.

### Transformation & State Change / 转化·状态迁移
Something becomes another state through a visible operation.
Article signals: "becomes", "turns into", "before/after", "from X to Y", "转化", "迁移", "修复后".
Visual encoding: before/after twins, hinge gate, folding station, repair seam, color shift, or carrier ribbon passing through a converter.
Operator family: Gate Builder / 闸门员, Care Folder / 照料员, Color Tuner / 调色员.
Precision ladder: before and after -> conversion path -> input state, operation, output state, residue, and state labels.
Degrades into: a magic arrow between two objects.

### Boundary, Filter & Threshold / 边界·过滤·门槛
Some items are allowed through, held back, softened, or transformed at an edge.
Article signals: "boundary", "filter", "threshold", "permission", "approval", "拒绝", "门槛", "筛选", "进入条件".
Visual encoding: gate, sieve, paper screen, threshold strip, stamp tray, quiet zone, or soft fence with pass/fail tags.
Operator family: Gate Builder / 闸门员, Boundary Keeper / 边界员, Valve Keeper / 阀门员.
Precision ladder: wall between sides -> gate with pass/fail -> named rule, blocked items, admitted items, and gate state.
Degrades into: a barrier that does not explain the rule.

### Divergence & Convergence / 分流·汇聚
One source splits into paths, or many sources merge into one outcome.
Article signals: "branches", "splits", "many paths", "converges", "synthesizes", "分流", "汇聚", "归一".
Visual encoding: forked blue carrier, grouped tags, funnel tray, braid, collector shelf, or braided threads becoming one strip.
Operator family: Weaver / 编织员, Cartographer / 制图员, Thread Runner / 牵线员.
Precision ladder: branching arrows -> labeled route split -> criteria at fork, path states, merged output, and grouped label set.
Degrades into: a tree diagram with no rule for branching or merging.

### Tension & Equilibrium / 张力·均衡
Forces hold each other in place; the relation is unresolved but stable or negotiated.
Article signals: "tension", "between", "held together", "not resolved", "dynamic balance", "拉扯", "僵持", "均衡".
Visual encoding: two pulled ribbons, counterweights, suspended frame, negotiated table, or balanced pressure tabs around one focal object.
Operator family: Mediator / 调停员, Tradeoff Weigher / 权衡员, Anchor Pin / 定位员.
Precision ladder: opposing sides -> visible force lines -> force labels, anchor point, equilibrium state, and what would break if one side wins.
Degrades into: two arrows pointing at each other.

## Precision Moves

- Direction markers: use small route tabs, arrow cuts, or thread knots; avoid giant arrows.
- Condition tags: attach "if / unless / after / except / 当..." tags to gates, latches, or thresholds.
- Before/after twins: show both states when the article claims transformation, repair, loss, or maturity.
- Counterweights: make tradeoffs physical with weights, removed cards, or tilted platforms.
- Broken-vs-mended path: show failure and repair on the same carrier when the claim is about recovery.
- Gate states: mark open, closed, half-open, rejected, filtered, or converted.
- Reinforcing-vs-balancing loop arrows: use growth tabs for reinforcing loops and dampers, valves, or scales for balancing loops.
- Primary/secondary layering: keep the main relation on the blue path; put side relations in quiet gray layers, amber evidence tags, coral risk tabs, or mint growth tabs.

## Composing Multiple Relations

When an article nests 2-3 relations, render the primary relation as the main path, the secondary as state coding, and the tertiary as a grouped label set. Keep one focal module.

Use `references/state-coding.md` for pass/fail, before/after, stable/unstable, hidden/visible, and risk/reward states. Use `references/primitives.md` for ribbon/path, gate, scale, frame, lens, shelf, layers, tags, and light primitives.

Do not give every relation equal visual weight. If every relation gets its own arrow system, the image becomes a diagram dump.

## Accept / Regenerate

Accept if:

- The primary relation is named before the operator is chosen.
- Direction, condition, and state are visible in objects, not only in labels.
- The operator physically performs the relation: hands off, compares, filters, weighs, anchors, braids, maps, reads, or mediates.
- The visual encoding is specific enough that replacing it with a plain arrow would lose meaning.
- Secondary relations are coded quietly and do not compete with the focal module.
- Labels are attached to relationship objects: path tabs, gate rules, scale weights, frame plaques, shelves, layers, or signal tags.

Regenerate if:

- The relationship collapses into a left-to-right arrow.
- Sequence, causality, and dependency are visually indistinguishable.
- Contrast becomes two unrelated panels.
- Feedback becomes a decorative loop.
- Tension resolves into a winner when the article says it is held in balance.
- The operator stands beside the relationship instead of changing, holding, reading, or regulating it.
