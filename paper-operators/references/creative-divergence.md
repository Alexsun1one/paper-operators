# Creative Divergence / 创意发散

Use this reference when the image has the right paper style but the metaphor
feels habitual.

The goal is not novelty. The goal is precision: find the metaphor world that
fits this source anchor, this relationship, and this reader intent better than
the defaults.

## Why Diverge

Prototype-lock is the root of the boring feeling.

The common locks are:

- route table
- gallery workbench
- soft boundary room

These worlds are useful, but they become weak when they appear before the
article has made its own demand.

Prototype-lock fails because:

- the metaphor fits Paper Operators more than the article
- the primary relationship collapses into a familiar layout
- domain-specific tools and textures disappear
- emotional temperature becomes generic calm
- the reader feels a template instead of an interpretation

Diverge first. Converge second.

Precision beats novelty. A familiar route is correct when the article is truly
about routing. A fresh metaphor is only better when its physical world renders
the relation more exactly.

## Divergence Method

Start from one source anchor.

```text
source anchor:
reader intent:
primary relationship:
domain:
emotional temperature:
default metaphor risk:
```

Generate 3-5 directions before writing the final prompt.

Each direction must change at least one angle:

- operator family
- domain lens
- physical metaphor world
- emotional temperature
- composition mode

Sketch each direction in one line.

```text
direction:
- metaphor world:
- operator family:
- relationship rendered:
- one-line scene:
- why it fits:
- risk:
```

Score quickly:

| Criterion | Question |
| --- | --- |
| Content precision | Does the world fit the specific source anchor? |
| Relationship clarity | Does it show direction, condition, and state? |
| Intent fit | Does it serve explain, feel, trust, decide, or remember? |
| Domain specificity | Does it belong to this topic's world? |
| Freshness | Does it escape defaults without becoming random? |
| Simplicity | Can the reader understand it in three seconds? |

Choose the direction with the strongest fit, not the strangest premise.

## Convergence Criteria

After divergence, converge hard. Do not keep five ideas alive in one image.

Pick the direction that is:

1. most precise to the specific content
2. most fit to the inferred intent
3. clearest about the primary relationship
4. most distinct from defaults already used
5. simplest without flattening the idea

Use `references/intent-reading.md` to infer whether the user wants explanation,
emotion, prestige, critique, persuasion, or memory.

Use `references/variation-engine.md` after the world is chosen: vary camera,
state coding, labels, operator expression, and material rhythm without changing
the core metaphor.

Use `references/composition-modes.md` after convergence to choose the layout:
single focal scene, one-map multi-step terrain, split-stage comparison, cutaway,
loop, or chained route.

## One-Map Multi-Step Rule

When several beats happen in one strategic field, do not automatically split
them into a series.

Use one-map multi-step composition when:

- the same terrain, table, system, room, or bench contains all steps
- the reader must compare steps spatially, not only chronologically
- the final beat changes the meaning of earlier beats
- a repeated path, loop, or crossing is the core idea

Good examples:

- four crossings on one river terrain
- three revisions on one manuscript table
- five negotiations around one shared pressure point
- a feedback loop returning to the same valve

Regenerate if it becomes four disconnected mini-panels. The point is one field
with several visible beats, not a comic strip disguised as a map.

## Accept / Regenerate

Accept if:

- 3-5 metaphor worlds were considered before the final prompt
- the chosen world fits the source anchor more precisely than the defaults
- the primary relationship is visible in objects, paths, and state coding
- domain tools, materials, palette, and labels belong to the article
- novelty never outruns clarity
- the composition has one focal module or one readable field
- the operator physically performs the relation inside the chosen world
- short labels are attached to objects, routes, gates, tools, or state markers

Regenerate if:

- the scene defaults to route-table / gallery / room without proving fit
- the metaphor is fresh but unrelated to the source anchor
- candidates differ only by decoration, not by relationship or domain lens
- the image reads as a flat chart with paper texture
- the operator is decorative after the metaphor world is chosen
- the user intent is ignored for a clever visual premise
- a multi-step composition is crowded, disconnected, or unreadable at a glance
