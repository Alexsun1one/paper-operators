# Domain Adaptation

Paper Operators must fit the article's world. Do not force engineering metaphors into every topic.

## Decision Order

1. Identify the article domain.
2. Identify the emotional temperature: analytical, warm, critical, melancholic, playful, urgent, elegant, intimate, or strategic.
3. Choose the operator's role and tool from that domain.
4. Choose one physical metaphor world.
5. Choose labels and colors that fit the article's seriousness.

## Domain Map

### Art, Design, Aesthetics

Use gallery, studio, frame, plinth, paper palette, color strips, light screens, crop marks, catalog cards, folded canvas, negative space.

Good actions: frame, tune, crop, hang, light, restore, layer.

Avoid: funnels, dashboards, security gates, generic flow arrows.

### Literature, Culture, History

Use archive drawers, footnotes, index cards, timelines, museum labels, repaired paper, stage curtains, memory shelves.

Good actions: file, unfold, compare, annotate, preserve, sequence.

Avoid: business roadmaps unless the article is actually strategic.

### Personal Essays, Psychology, Life

Use folded notes, chairs, small rooms, weather cards, thread circles, soft screens, envelopes, windows, repaired paper edges.

Good actions: shelter, carry, fold, mend, separate, wait, place.

Avoid: over-explaining feelings as charts.

### Business, Strategy, Product

Use tables, routes, gates, scales, stacks, maps, funnels, score cards, decision stamps, stage boards.

Good actions: route, filter, weigh, stack, stamp, compare, bridge.

Avoid: soulless dashboard card walls and generic KPI icons.

### Engineering, AI, Systems

Use carriers, nodes, cutaways, gates, lenses, traces, logs, loops, handoff ribbons, failure bins.

Good actions: inspect, route, block, repair, reveal, isolate, reconnect.

Avoid: robot heads, glowing brains, circuit wallpaper, abstract "AI magic."

### Education, Learning, Thinking

Use shelves, notebooks, ladders, layer cards, practice trays, before/after boards, concept blocks.

Good actions: stack, sort, compare, distill, arrange, repair.

Avoid: classroom clipart and checkmark-under-every-step courseware.

### Food, Lifestyle, Craft

Use kitchen table, recipe cards, fabric, trays, folded wrappers, jars, soft labels, cutting boards.

Good actions: mix, fold, sort, carry, label, preserve.

Avoid: over-engineered diagrams unless the article is analytical.

### Finance, Law, Policy

Use ledgers, stamps, scales, gates, thresholds, folders, sealed envelopes, compliance trays.

Good actions: weigh, stamp, seal, filter, compare, audit.

Avoid: playful cuteness that weakens seriousness.

## Label Strategy

Use model-rendered short labels by default. A Paper Operators image should not feel empty or wordless.

Default:

- 3-6 short labels for simple figures
- more labels when the article needs a multi-state, multi-path, comparison, or grouped figure
- 2-6 Chinese characters per Chinese label where possible
- labels attached to objects
- one readable sentence strip is allowed when it makes the takeaway clearer
- no dense paragraphs
- no tiny background pseudo-text

Use blank label containers for:

- explicit post-production overlay requests
- dense Chinese text
- legal, financial, or client-critical wording
- titles or cover text
- anything that must be editable

Good labels are attached to objects:

- tabs
- stamps
- frame labels
- path markers
- tray labels
- cards
- plinth captions
- hanging paper tags
- gallery plaques
- stitched labels
- small route signs

Avoid floating text and bottom principle bars.

## Aerial Scene Choices

If the user references a polished high-angle paper illustration, choose one of these scene forms before prompting:

- **Aerial route table**: idea card -> operator action stations -> final article object.
- **Gallery workbench**: scraps -> frame/light action -> focused artwork.
- **Soft boundary room**: note pile -> paper screen -> breathing room.
- **Archive table**: scattered evidence -> sorting tray -> lineage shelf.
- **Miniature town**: abstract system -> operator route -> small spatial outcome.

Use only one form per image. Keep the main route visible and smooth.
