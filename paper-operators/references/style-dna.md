# Paper Operators Style DNA / 纸片人风格 DNA

Use this reference before final image generation.

Paper Operators is a tactile paper-stage illustration system for article body images. Its identity comes from faceless folded-paper figures performing conceptual actions inside a clean editorial diagram.

## Chinese-First Default

When the user writes Chinese, plan and describe the image in Chinese. The default image must include readable Chinese labels, not empty label placeholders. For publish-critical wording, the model may use shorter labels and the final answer should note any text that may need manual overlay.

Default label rule:

- Use 3-6 short labels for simple figures.
- Use more labels when the article truly needs a multi-state, multi-path, comparison, or grouped diagram.
- Each Chinese label should usually be 2-6 characters.
- Attach labels to objects, states, paths, tabs, frames, shelves, gates, or plinths.
- A single readable sentence strip is allowed when it clarifies the takeaway; do not use dense paragraphs, tiny fake text, or empty labels as the default.
- Use blank label containers only when the user explicitly asks for post-production overlay or editable text.

Good Chinese label containers:

- stitched paper tags
- gallery plaques
- hanging labels
- paper tabs
- small cards attached to trays, frames, paths, shelves, gates, or plinths

Avoid floating captions, dense fake Chinese, and text-empty placeholder scenes.

## Core Look

- 16:9 horizontal by default.
- Warm off-white paper background, subtle paper grain only if it helps tactile quality.
- Paper-cut or folded-paper objects, light shadows, calm premium composition.
- Charcoal or ink outlines with slight hand-built imperfection, not messy doodle.
- Small semantic color tabs on the operator or tool.
- Main colors stay restrained: ink, paper, quiet gray, passage blue, evidence amber, risk coral, growth mint, plus one focus accent when needed.
- Use one paper operator unless the concept truly needs a relationship between two operators.
- The operator is usually 8-18% of canvas height.
- The image should work inside an article column, not only as a poster.

## Aerial Paper-Stage Mode

Use this mode when the user asks for "more beautiful", "more three-dimensional", "like the reference image", "high-angle", "paper model", "立体", "高空视角", or "线条流畅".

Rules:

- Use a high-angle or shallow isometric tabletop view.
- Build a miniature paper diorama, not a flat poster.
- Make every concept a tangible paper object: card, tray, gate, lens, frame, lamp, shelf, room, town, route, plinth, archive drawer, or tool board.
- Use one flowing blue carrier ribbon as the reader path when the image has a process or transformation.
- Let complexity match the article. Simple claims should stay simple; complex relationships may use more objects, states, and paths when the labels, routes, and state coding stay clear.
- Make labels beautiful as objects: tabs, plaques,吊牌, stitched labels, gallery captions, route markers.
- Include readable labels by default. Simple figures use a few short labels; complex figures may use more grouped labels and one sentence strip if readability remains high. Use blank label containers only as an explicit overlay mode.
- Let the paper operator touch the carrier, lens, gate, frame, light card, folder, room screen, or scale. Do not let the operator stand beside the scene.

Reject if it becomes:

- a PPT flowchart
- equal-weight dashboard cards
- a flat icon grid
- a busy miniature city with no visual argument
- a poster with decorative paper texture but no actionable paper operator
- a pretty scene with no readable text

## Paper Person Rules

The operator:

- has no eyes, mouth, brows, hair, face, or facial expression
- has a folded tile or hood-like head
- has a simple paper torso made from rounded rectangles, tiles, capsules, wedges, arcs, or strips
- may have simple limbs only to perform the action
- carries one tool that matches the article domain
- is secondary to the idea but essential to the relation

The tool does the storytelling. The body stays quiet.

## Not Xiaohei

This skill may learn from Xiaohei's workflow mechanism: a recurring figure must do the conceptual work. It must not copy Xiaohei's visual identity.

Do not use:

- black blob body
- white-dot eyes
- tiny thin legs
- deadpan monster shape
- whiteboard doodle identity
- a small black worker repeated across every image
- Xiaohei composition replicas

## Not Mascot

Do not make the paper person:

- cute-first
- expressive
- waving, smiling, presenting, or standing beside the diagram
- a sticker
- a logo character
- a generic office worker
- a robot, avatar, head icon, or silhouette

## World-Class Quality Bar

Accept only if:

- the idea reads in three seconds
- the paper operator physically changes, reveals, routes, repairs, filters, tunes, balances, or reframes something
- the composition has a clear focal module and generous quiet zones
- labels are short and attached to objects, tabs, frames, paths, or surfaces
- the domain feels specific, not engineering props pasted onto every article
- the image feels editorial, tactile, and publishable

Reject if:

- the operator is decorative
- the image looks like a PPT infographic
- it uses stock icons
- it has fake dense text
- it is charming but unclear
- it is accurate but visually lifeless
